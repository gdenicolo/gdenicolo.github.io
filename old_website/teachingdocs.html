SUPPORT TEACHING MATERIAL: PHYSICS @ SCCC
Dear instructor: please do not feel obliged to use all material presented here, you can always exercise your academic freedom. These are the lab instructions & course outlines that I & colleagues have used for the courses below (always subject to revision every time we teach these courses again). This material is here with the only purpose of helping you to figure out your own class plan. If you would like to see more material (namely, extra-credit assignments, homeworks, tests that I have used, DOC instead of PDF) please drop me an e-mail (denicog@sunysuffolk.edu). My own personal course websites are linked above. Notice that as of Fall/14, SCCC is using Blackboard as repository for course material (exclusive access to students & their faculty).
 
Disclaimer: The material here is intended for SCCC students only. G. Denicolo does not claim authorship of the lab material. Most lab instructions are extracted from Warasila et al. lab manual and Vernier lab instructions. Some other ideas are excerpts from the web (source is indicated whenever possible). Therefore, because of copyrights concerns, this website is kept private (there are no external links to it).

PHY 101 : College Physics I

Algebra-Based Physics, Mechanics (4 credits). Link to general SCCC syllabus for PHY101.

PHY101 example of past course outlines: as a night class, as a day class. Current textbook (as of Fall/14): Serway & Vuille, Cengage, 9th ed.
  • Force diagram exercises: In-class powerpoint presentation and accompanied handout for students.
PHY101 LAB INSTRUCTIONS: students are asked to record instrumental uncertainties, and calculate percentage differences for most experiments. Some instructions on how to write formal lab reports are presented at the end of the "ball toss" lab.
  • General lab instructions
  • Introduction to lab measurements
  • One dimensional horizontal motion (x versus t)
  • One dimensional horizontal motion (v versus t)
  • One dimensional vertical motion (ball toss, get g)
  • Motion on an inclined plane (get g)
  • Two dimensional motion (projectile motion)
  • Newton's second law (air tracks)
  • Newton's second law (force sensor and accelerometer)
  • Coefficient of static and kinetic friction (force sensor)
  • Work and energy (force sensor and motion detector)
  • Work and energy (air tracks)
  • Impulse and momentum (force sensor and motion detector)
  • Types of collisions (air tracks)
  • More collisions (two motion detectors, two carts)
  • Determining the centripetal force (special apparatus)

PHY 102 : College Physics II

Algebra-Based Physics: Waves, Optics, Electromag, Thermo (4 credits). Link to general SCCC syllabus for PHY102.

PHY102 example of past course outlines: here, and here. Current textbook (as of Fall/14): Serway & Vuille, Cengage, 9th ed.
 
PHY102 LAB INSTRUCTIONS: students are asked to record instrumental uncertainties, and calculate percentage differences for most experiments. All lab instructions below belong to Warasila et al. manual (lswpubrw@optonline.net).
  • Young's Modulus
  • Simple Harmonic Motion
  • Standing waves on a string
  • Wave equation
  • Superposition of sound waves
  • Speed of sound in air
  • Specific heat of metals
  • Latent heat of fusion
  • Mechanical equivalent of heat
  • Sources of emf
  • Electric field mapping
  • Ohm's law
  • DC circuits
  • Magnetic force on a current-carrying conductor
  • Measuring a background magnetic field
  • e/m measurement for the electron
  • Geometric optics of simple lenses and mirrors
  • Diffraction

PHY 132: Physics I Lab

Calculus-Based Physics Laboratory, Mechanics (1 credit). This course is designed to complement and illustrate the concepts learned during the lecture course taken in parallel (PHY130). Link to general SCCC syllabus for PHY132.

PHY132 example of past course outline. Current textbook for PHY130 (as of Fall/14): Giancoli, Pearson, 4th ed.
 
PHY132 LAB INSTRUCTIONS: students are asked to record instrumental uncertainties, calculate standard deviation of repeated measurements, and perform error propagation (formulas from differential calculus) for most experiments. Some instructions on how to write formal lab reports are presented at the end of the "ball toss" lab.
  • General lab instructions
  • Introduction to lab measurements
  • One dimensional horizontal motion (x and v versus t)
  • One dimensional vertical motion (ball toss, get g)
  • Motion on an incline (get g)
  • Two dimensional motion (projectile motion)
  • Newton's second law (air tracks)
  • Coefficient of static and kinetic friction (force sensor)
  • Work and energy (force sensor and motion detector)
  • Impulse and momentum (force sensor and motion detector)
  • Conservation of momentum (air tracks)
  • Collisions and Energy (two motion detectors, two carts)
  • Determining the centripetal force (special apparatus)

PHY 115: Technical Physics for Health Careers

Physics for Health Careers (4 credits). Link to general SCCC syllabus for PHY115.

PHY115 LAB INSTRUCTIONS: this group of students used to consist of future radiology and ultrasound technicians. The math pre-requisite is very basic MAT007 (Algebra I), therefore I found that hands-on practice (i.e., labs) can be more successful than lecturing for this group.
  • One dimensional horizontal motion (x versus t)
  • One dimensional horizontal motion (v versus t)
  • Two dimensional motion (projectile motion)
  • One dimensional vertical motion (ball toss, get g)
  • Newton's second law (force sensor and accelerometer)
  • Centripetal force and acceleration (special apparatus)
  • Energy of tossed ball (motion detector)
  • Work (force sensor and motion detector)
  • Arquimedes and buoyancy
  • Electric circuits
  • Build the simplest electromagnetic motor
  • Determine the speed of sound (microphone and tube)
  • Sounds and beats (microphone and tuning forks)
  • Optics
