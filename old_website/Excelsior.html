Currently we do not use WebAssign to assign online homeworks to our students, but there is growing opinion that online homeworks are in our future. I think we have resisted so far because:
  • this is a community college, and therefore we do our best to keep the costs as low as possible for the students;
  • we have a maximum limit of 24 students per class, in contrast to universities that have > 400 students per class.
However, these are some points that we at SCCC see in favor of assigning online homework:
  • there is a growing number of students asking for more studying options, such as online tutoring and online consultation (online homeworks can provide hints to solve a problem, and can be set up to explain the answer at the end);
  • even with only 24 students per class, it is unrealistic to grade homeworks on a weekly basis. Our physics faculty can teach about 100 students per semester, and all work is corrected and graded by us - we do not have teaching assistants.

My Documents

Most of my course material is now placed inside our private D2L WEBSITE, which allows for private access by SCCC students only. A great advantage of D2L ("Desire-2-Learn") is that students can see all their grades and keep track of their final grade calculation at all times during the semester. This increases student's accountability and responsibility for their own performance.
