Poster


Author: SCCC student Brittney Herold; advisor: G. Denicoló

Where/when: SUNY Annual Undergraduate Research Conference (SURC), April 22, 2017.

Title: The physics of martial arts

Abstract: Since the beginning of martial arts biomechanics research, scientists have been interested in quantifying the speed and forces involved in strikes performed by highly trained practitioners. Video analysis is used to study two martial arts movements: a punch and a kick. We recorded the movements done by the author (a trained practitioner of the Korean martial art Soo Bahk Do) with a video camera at a rate of 120 frames per second. LoggerPro Vernier software was used to trace the motion of leg and hand frame-by-frame. Velocity and acceleration were graphed in order to obtain values commonly cited in motor skills literature: maximum speed, force, momentum, duration of impact, energy and power. We present our measurements, and discuss their uncertainties. The uncertainties in video analysis stem from projection effects in the calibration of the frame dimension, and the limited resolution of the objects (hand and foot) used to trace positions across frames. One of our main findings is that maximum velocity, force and power are achieved before contact with target, a fact that has been reported in literature and is not ideal in optimal performance of any sport activity. Finally, we present a comparison with literature values and discuss the differences.

Poster

Where/when:Astronomical Society of New York (ASNY), Nov 4, 2014.

Title: Nova KT Eri: a detailed analysis of emission lines

Abstract: Emission lines in the early-stage spectrum of novae (i.e., few days after maximum) are often heavily blended, even in high resolution observations, hindering the study of individual line behavior. We have modeled over twenty emission lines using a chi-square minimization routine, focusing in the early-stage optical spectra of KT Eri (maximum on Nov 15, 2009). These are preliminary results on the time evolution of several lines from day +31 to +160 after maximum. The relation between the onset of the supersoft X-ray emission and optical emission lines is also investigated.

I am the current associate member institution representative at ASNY for SCCC.
