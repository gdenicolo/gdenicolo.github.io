Exploring the creation of PHY100: Preparatory Physics

An 8-week, 2hrs per week, Pass/Fail course, worth 1 credit, split into 8 modules (1 module per week) enforcing the following topics:
Week/Module Content with physics applications
1 Units, powers of 10, scientific notation
2 Linear equations, factoring
3 Equation of a straight line, graphical representation, slope and y-intercept
4 Quadratic equation, solving it, and its graphical representation including sign of curvature
5 System of linear equations: two unknown variables
6 Right triangle: sine, cosine, tangent, Pythagoras
7 Apply trigonometry to project vectors in x- and y-axes
8 Vector algebra: one- and two-dimension vectors
The initial idea is that PHY100 will not be a required course, but there will be a diagnostic test for all prospective PHY103, 105, 118, 121, 130 students (all students who register for those courses will be sent a link to take the diagnostic test), advising whether or not they should take PHY100 before attempting an entry-level physics course. PHY100 could be offered in the summer and/or during the last 8 weeks of the Fall and Spring semesters. Students failing one of the entry physics courses during Fall or Spring, and whose diagnostic test previously advised them to take PHY100 beforehand, would be given the option to switch to PHY100 without cost by mid-semester. In order to be in good standing (i.e. to pass PHY100), students should pass at least 5 out of 8 modules in this manner:
Desired/intended course Must pass at least five PHY100 modules
PHY103 or PHY105 necessarily including 2, 3, 4
PHY118 necessarily including 5, 6, 7, 8
PHY130 necessarily including 5, 6, 7, 8
